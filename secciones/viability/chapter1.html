My Role in the Prefeasibility Stage

In this stage I applied mining-industry and PMI-based practices to structure the Prefeasibility Study of an Industrial Water Treatment Plant (250 L/s, expandable to 350 L/s) in the Andes Mining Corridor (Apurímac, Peru). My work covered scope definition, technical criteria, risk analysis, preliminary schedule, and CAPEX/OPEX estimates.

  • Defined the preliminary project scope and drafted the project charter for the new WTP.
  • Structured the prefesibility stage using a mining-industry checklist (Project Management for Mining, Ch. 7).
  • Developed the initial process concept, including mass balance and a long-list of major equipment.
  • Prepared the environmental red-flag scan, preliminary permit map, and social constraints overview.
  • Issued a Class 4 (+/−25%) CAPEX estimate, preliminary OPEX estimate, and a Level 2 schedule.
  • Built the first risk register and recommended go/no-go conditions to move to Feasibility.

Prefeasibility Workflow – Industrial WTP (250 L/s)

Summary of how I structured the Prefeasibility Study, inspired by Flowchart 7.1 of Project Management for Mining.

  • 1. Management Review: confirmation of business need and assignment of Project Manager for the WTP.
  • 2. Team Setup: definition of core team and external specialists (process, environmental, geotech, cost).
  • 3. Data Collection: site information, water quality data, production plans, existing infrastructure.
  • 4. Data Analysis & Concepts: process alternatives, mass balance, infrastructure options, constraints.
  • 5. Environmental / Permits / Social: red-flag review, permit path, social baseline and issues.
  • 6. Risk Evaluation: first risk register and contingency philosophy.
  • 7. Schedule & Cost: Level 2 schedule, CAPEX Class 4, OPEX estimate, economic indicators (NPV/IRR at this stage if applicable).
  • 8. Prefeasibility Report: integrated summary of scope, technical concept, risks, and economics.
  • 9. Management Decision: go/no-go to Feasibility, with clear conditions and critical investigations for the next stage.

Conceptual Process Flow Diagram (PFD)

High-level conceptual flow diagram for the Industrial Water Treatment Plant (250 L/s). This diagram represents the treatment barriers considered at prefeasibility level.

Process Stage Descriptions

  • Raw Water Intake: Initial capture of raw industrial or mine-contact water. Includes screens or coarse filtration, flow measurement, and pumping to the pretreatment system.
  • Coagulation / Flocculation: Rapid mixing of coagulant chemicals to destabilize suspended solids, followed by slow mixing to form larger flocs that can be separated in the clarifier.
  • Clarification: Sedimentation process where flocculated solids settle out. Reduces turbidity and suspended solids, producing clarified water suitable for downstream multimedia filtration.
  • Multimedia Filtration (MMF): Pressure or gravity sand filter that removes remaining particulates. Serves as final polishing step before membrane protection.
  • Ultrafiltration (UF): Membrane barrier with pore sizes 0.01–0.1 μm. Provides high-quality effluent with very low turbidity, bacteria removal, and consistent feed water for RO.
  • Reverse Osmosis (RO): High-pressure membrane separation that removes dissolved salts, metals, and organics. Produces high-quality treated water suitable for process reuse, while generating a concentrated reject stream.
  • Treated Water: Final permeate meeting industrial water specifications. Can be reused in process, cooling systems, or sent to storage for distribution.

1. Prefeasibility Scope Statement

Objective: define the technical, environmental, and economic feasibility of constructing a 250 L/s Industrial Water Treatment Plant for mine process water reuse, with future debottlenecking to 350 L/s.

Scope Includes:

  • Evaluation of water sources, raw water quality envelope, and required treated water quality.
  • Conceptual process design for the WTP (intake, pre-treatment, main treatment, polishing, sludge management).
  • Definition of plot plan envelope and tie-ins to existing mine infrastructure (power, roads, control, utilities).
  • Preliminary CAPEX Class 4 and OPEX estimates for the selected concept.
  • High-level Level 2 schedule from authorization to commissioning.
  • Identification of key permitting, environmental, and social constraints.
  • Initial risk register and go/no-go recommendation to move into Feasibility Study (FS).

2. Site & Context Analysis

High-level analysis of location, logistics, and physical constraints for the WTP in the Andes Mining Corridor.

  • Characterized the project location (altitude, climate, seismicity, access roads, geotechnical context).
  • Defined candidate plant sites within the mine area and screened them against topography and existing facilities.
  • Mapped water sources (contact water, non-contact water, process effluents) and potential reuse destinations.
  • Analysed logistics for construction materials, large equipment delivery, and chemical storage.
  • Identified site constraints (right-of-way, existing tailings, safety distances, operational interferences).
  • Produced a first Context & Constraints Map as input to process concept selection and layout.

3. Environmental, Permits, Social & Sustainability

Early-stage review of environmental and social conditions to avoid fatal flaws and frame permit strategy.

  • Reviewed applicable environmental regulations for industrial water treatment and discharge/reuse.
  • Prepared a red-flag checklist (protected areas, water bodies, communities, indigenous lands, cultural sites).
  • Outlined the permit path (EIA amendments, water rights, discharge permits, waste/slag management, construction permits).
  • Identified baseline information gaps (hydrology, water quality, biodiversity, social baseline).
  • Drafted a preliminary social constraints map (stakeholders, potential community concerns, access roads shared with locals).
  • Summarized sustainability opportunities (energy efficiency, chemical optimization, sludge valorization, circular water use).

4. Process Concept & Design Basis

Conceptual process design for a modular WTP able to treat variable mine water quality while meeting reuse specs.

  • Defined design flows (average, peak, and expansion case) and operating philosophy (24/7, redundancy criteria).
  • Established the design basis for raw water quality (TSS, metals, sulphates, hardness, pH, temperature, etc.).
  • Developed and compared process alternatives (e.g., clarification + filtration + RO vs. high-rate clarification + UF + RO).
  • Selected a preferred process train and produced a conceptual Process Flow Diagram (PFD).
  • Prepared a preliminary mass balance including brine, sludge, and by-product streams.
  • Compiled an equipment long-list (major pumps, clarifiers, filters, membranes, blowers, tanks).
  • Documented design criteria (recovery, turndown, redundancy, availability targets, control strategy).

5. Infrastructure & Utilities

High-level definition of supporting infrastructure required to build and operate the WTP within the mine site.

  • Outlined the plot plan envelope for the WTP, including access roads, laydown, and safety distances.
  • Defined tie-ins to mine power system (medium-voltage feeders, substations, emergency power philosophy).
  • Estimated utility demands (power, compressed air, instrument air, backwash water, potable water).
  • Defined chemical storage and dosing areas (coagulants, flocculants, acids, alkalis, antiscalants, etc.).
  • Identified sludge management options (dewatering, storage, final disposal route or co-disposal with mine tailings).
  • Summarized control and automation interfaces with the mine’s central control room (SCADA/DCS).

6. CAPEX Estimate – Class 4 (+/−25%)

Capital cost estimate structured according to mining-industry practices and Project Management for Mining (Ch. 7).

  • Structured the estimate using a WBS separating process units, buildings, infrastructure, and indirects.
  • Applied quantity take-offs from conceptual layout and typical sections (civil, mechanical, piping, electrical).
  • Used a mix of factored estimates, historical benchmarks, and budgetary vendor quotes for main equipment.
  • Included engineering, procurement, and construction management (EPCM) indirect costs.
  • Allocated contingency based on design maturity, data quality, and risk profile (Class 4 level).
  • Presented CAPEX by major cost centres and by WBS level 2 for integration with CBS/EVM later.

7. Operating Cost (OPEX) Estimate

Order-of-magnitude operating cost for the selected process concept, aligned with the CAPEX Class 4 accuracy.

  • Estimated power consumption based on major equipment loads and operating profiles.
  • Calculated chemical consumption from mass balance and design doses (coagulants, flocculants, antiscalants, etc.).
  • Defined staffing model (shifts, operators, maintenance technicians, supervision) and labour cost.
  • Included maintenance and spare parts budgets based on equipment type and duty.
  • Estimated sludge disposal costs for different disposal or co-disposal scenarios.
  • Summarized OPEX in $/(m³ treated) and $/(m³ reused) for decision-making.

8. Level 2 Schedule – Prefeasibility to Commissioning

High-level schedule describing the main phases, durations, and interfaces for the WTP project.

  • Defined schedule structure by phases: Prefeasibility, Feasibility, Basic Engineering, Detailed Engineering, Procurement, Construction, Commissioning.
  • Built a precedence diagram with FS/SS relationships and rough durations for each major activity.
  • Identified a preliminary critical path (permits → detailed engineering → long-lead equipment → construction).
  • Incorporated long-lead items (membranes, clarifiers, large pumps) based on vendor lead times.
  • Synchronized the WTP schedule with mine expansion milestones to ensure water is available before production ramp-up.
  • Generated a Level 2 Gantt ready to be refined into Level 3 during Feasibility.

9. Risk Register & Go/No-Go Recommendation

First risk view of the WTP project, aligned with the mining company’s risk framework and the Prefeasibility checklist.

  • Identified technical risks (raw water variability, membrane fouling, sludge handling capacity).
  • Captured schedule risks (permitting timelines, long-lead equipment, access road works, tie-ins).
  • Included cost risks (steel price volatility, FX exposure, vendor concentration, logistics).
  • Assessed environmental/social risks (community opposition to infrastructure, discharge routes, land use).
  • Scored risks qualitatively (probability x impact) and suggested mitigation strategies.
  • Issued a concise go/no-go recommendation and conditions to move to Feasibility (data to be collected, studies to be completed).
